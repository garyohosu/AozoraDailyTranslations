Asakusa Park

by Akutagawa, Ryunosuke · 2026-03-10

A modern English translation of the opening numbered sequence of Ryunosuke Akutagawa's "Asakusa Park," preserving its cinematic, dreamlike shifts in perspective and atmosphere. (QA warning)

1

The great paper lantern hanging inside Asakusa's Niomon gate is unlit. Gradually it rises higher and higher, until it seems to overlook the crowded Nakamise. Only the bottom of the lantern does not disappear. In front of the gate, countless pigeons flutter through the air.

2

The Nakamise seen lengthwise from the Kaminarimon Gate. Far ahead, straight in front, Niomon can be seen. The trees are all bare.

3

One side of the Nakamise. A man in an overcoat is strolling along with a boy of twelve or thirteen. The boy lets go of his father's hand and now and then stops in front of toy shops. Of course the father does scold him from time to time. But sometimes he himself seems to forget the boy is even there, pausing to look into a hat shop's display window.

4

The upper bodies of this father and son. The father is plainly a country fellow, with unshaven whiskers. The boy's face is not so much cute as delicate. Behind them is the bustling Nakamise. They are walking toward us.

5

A toy shop seen at an angle. The boy has stopped in front of it, gazing at a toy monkey that climbs up and down a rope. No one can be seen inside the shop. The boy is visible only down to the knees.

6

The monkey climbing up and down the rope. Its coattails hang behind it, and it wears a silk hat tipped back on its head. Behind the rope and the monkey there is nothing but deep darkness.

7

One side of the Nakamise where the toy shop stands. The boy, who had been watching the monkey, suddenly notices that his father is gone and begins looking around wildly. Then he spots something in the distance and runs headlong toward it.

8

The back of a man who seems to be his father, again visible only down to the knees. The boy catches up with him and grabs his overcoat sleeve tightly. But when the startled man turns around, his face is not that of the country-bred father after all. He is an urbane gentleman, with a neatly groomed mustache. Disappointment and confusion pass over the boy's face. Leaving the boy behind, the gentleman hurries on. With the distant Kaminarimon at his back, the boy stands there blankly alone.

9

Once more, the back of a man who seems to be his father, but this time only the upper body. The boy catches up to him and timidly looks up at his face. Beyond them is Niomon.

10

The man's face, seen from the front. Behind the mask that covers his mouth, his face looks less human than animal. A smile with something malicious in it.

11

One side of the Nakamise. The boy stands there, watching the man go, utterly at a loss. Whichever way he looks, his father's figure is nowhere to be seen. After thinking for a moment, he begins to walk aimlessly, not noticing that two girls in Western dress turn to look back at him.

12

The display window of an optician's shop. Among the rows of eyeglasses, telescopes, binoculars, magnifying lenses, microscopes, and dust goggles, there is the head of a Western doll, smiling through a pair of spectacles. In front of the window stands the boy, seen obliquely from behind, upper body only. The doll's head naturally changes into a human head. And more than that, it speaks to the boy:

13

"Buy a pair of glasses and put them on. There's nothing like glasses for finding your father, you know."

"My eyes aren't sick."

14

The display window of an artificial-flower shop seen at an angle. The flowers are all blooming out of bamboo baskets and ceramic pots. The largest of them is a tiger lily on the left. The plate glass of the window begins to reflect the boy's upper body, dimly, like a ghost.

15

The boy's upper body, seen through the plate glass beyond the artificial flowers. He has placed a hand against the glass. Before long, perhaps because his breath touches it, only his face grows clouded over.

16

The tiger lily in the display window. Behind it is darkness. The bud hanging beneath the flower gradually begins to open.

17

"Look at my beauty."

"But you're only an artificial flower, aren't you?"

18

The display window of a tobacconist seen from the corner. Among tins of cigarettes, boxes of cigars, and pipes, a placard hangs at an angle. Written on it are the words: "Tobacco smoke is the gate of heaven." Smoke rises slowly from a pipe.

19

The front of the display window, now filled with smoke. The boy stands on the right, again visible only down to the knees. Within the smoke, three castles gradually begin to float into view. They resemble a three-dimensional version of the Three Castles trademark.

20

One of those castles. At its gate stands a soldier holding a rifle. Beyond the iron-barred gate, several palm trees sway.

21

Above the castle gate. Before long, these words begin to appear there:

"Whoever enters this gate shall become a hero."

22

The boy walking toward us. The display window of the tobacconist stands diagonally behind him. He glances back once, then briskly walks on again.

23

Inside a bell tower where only the hanging bell can be seen. Someone pulls the rope attached to the striker, and slowly the bell begins to toll. Once, twice, three times. Outside the bell tower there are only pine trees.

24

A shooting gallery seen at an angle. Behind the targets are stacks of cigarette boxes; in front are Hakata dolls. In the foreground is a row of air rifles. One of the dolls is a Western woman in a dress, holding a fan. The boy timidly enters the stall, picks up an air rifle, and aims at the targets with complete recklessness. No one is in the shooting gallery. The boy is visible only down to the knees.

25

The doll of the Western woman. Quietly she opens her fan and completely hides her face. Then a cork bullet strikes her. Naturally the doll topples over backward. Behind the doll too there is nothing but darkness.

26

The same shooting gallery. The boy picks up the air rifle again, and this time he aims earnestly at the targets. Three shots, four shots, five shots. But not a single target falls. Reluctantly he takes out a silver coin and leaves the stall.

27

At first, in the dimness, only a square object can be seen. Then suddenly it seems to light up with an electric lamp, and these words appear across it: above, "Park Sixth District"; below, "Night Watch Station." The upper words are white on black, the lower red on black.

28

The upper rear of a theater. One lit window can be seen. On the wall, where a rainspout runs straight down, are the peeled-off traces of many posters.

29

The lower rear of this theater. The boy stands there, unwilling for a while to go either way. Then he looks up at the high window. But no one can be seen there. Only a sturdy bull terrier passes by at the boy's feet, sniffing at his scent.

30

The upper rear of the same theater. A dancer appears at the lit window and coldly looks down at the street below. Because of the backlighting, of course, her face cannot be clearly made out. But before long it takes on a delicate face resembling the boy's. Quietly the dancer opens the window and tosses a small bouquet down below.

31

At the boy's feet on the street. A small bouquet falls there. The boy's hand picks it up. But no sooner has the bouquet left the street than it has changed into a bundle of thorns.

32

A black notice board. Written on it in chalk are the words: "North wind, fair." But they grow dim and change into: "Strong south wind expected. Rain likely."

33

A signboard peddler's stall seen at an angle. The samples lined up beneath the awning bear such names as Tokugawa Ieyasu, Ninomiya Sontoku, Watanabe Kazan, Kondo Isami, and Chikamatsu Monzaemon. Before long these names too change into ordinary, commonplace names. More than that, beyond those signboards a pumpkin patch faintly begins to appear...

34

Several movie theaters lined up beyond a pond. Naturally the reflections of many electric lights shimmer in the water. The upper body of the boy standing to the left of the pond. In an instant, a gust of wind blows his cap into the pond. After flustering about in various ways, the boy turns and begins to walk toward us, his expression close to despair.

35

A cafe display window. Beyond a tower of sugar, pastries, and glasses of soda water with striped paper straws, several human figures move about. The boy comes past this window and stops to the left of it. He is visible only down to the knees.

36

The exterior of this cafe. A middle-aged man and woman, apparently husband and wife, go in through the glass door. The woman is a child wearing a mantle...
